Rules & Behavior

Do not ignite your lightsaber inside the temple unless there's an immediate danger.

Team killing is strictly forbidden under any circumstances, except when two individuals are participating in a consensual duel.

All forms of racism, toxicity, and hate are strictly banned. Always conduct yourself with maturity and seriousness. There is no place for childish behavior, as you must uphold the dignity of the Jedi Order. Show respect not only to those in higher ranks but also to everyone within the Jedi Order, as we are all a part of this Order. Temple Guardians must be treated with the highest respect, regardless of your rank within the Jedi Order. They are to be honored.

Do not interfere if a Temple Guard is fighting a hostile, and do not speak to the Temple Guards during such situations.

Rank Guide

Force Sensitives must complete an Introduction training to become a Hopeful I. Hopeful I must complete Combat Training and will teach you the basics of the combat system to advance to Hopeful II. Hopeful II must complete Lore Training this training will give information about lores that have happened to become a Youngling. Younglings must pass Crystal Gathering training to be ranked as an Initiate. Crystal Gathering is where you travel to the planet Ilum and enter a cave to find your very own Kyber Crystal, which you will use to build your own Lightsaber—a very old tradition in the Jedi Order. After completing Crystal Gathering, you can either find a Jedi Knight to train you or join the Jedi Academy, where you will be trained to become a Jedi Knight. You can find the Jedi Academy in the #Information channel on our server.

Welcome to this Combat Training, I’m your host [USERNAME], refer to me as [RANK].

Today, you will learn about the keybinds for the combat system, and we will have a training evaluation at the end of the session.

Arethere any questions before we begin ?

To start with, I will start with the keybinds. 1 = Equip Q = Ignite M1 = Attack M2 = Block E = Dash CTRL = Force Flip

Now, we will learn how to parry.

Parrying involves blocking for 1-2 seconds, which will stun your opponent and give you the opportunity to land a hit.

Pair up the attendees. If there is only one attendee, you or your co-host will allow them to practice parrying against you.

You are going to be parrying first.

You will be attacking first. The person who is parrying should remain still and attempt to block the attacker’s strikes. After 3 successful parries, switch roles.

Stand in front of me once more. Now, we will begin the combat evaluation. To pass, you must win at least one duel. Each of you will duel twice. You will face your partner in parrying. Good luck.

Guide them to either side of the center mat in the courtyard, instruct them to bow, ignite their sabers, and then begin the duel as usual. After the second duel: You may offer your attendees any combat advice you have learned. (Optional) If both of them pass: Congratulations to both of you; you have successfully completed the combat evaluation! If only one of them passes, say to the one who did not pass: I'm sorry, but you have not passed this combat training. Better luck next time. You are dismissed.

Thank you for attending this Combat training. I will log you all now. You will receive your ranking once a Jedi Master reviews the logs. You are dismissed and are free to leave.
